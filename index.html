What is -good design-?

I think objectively good design acts as a subtle prod at the our sense of novelty, gradually pushing our horizons of how design, aesthetic, and enviroment can present themselves to us, meeting efficiency in the middle.

Design can evoke in the observer near-ineffable reflections and emotions; stirring in the consumer their own personal experiences and frame of reference, and that power is vested in even the most banal picture or product, whispering in the language only art can speak, beckoning you to examine both yourself and the work.

With that being said! The real mettle of design is its ability to compromise to the context and user it conversely finds itself confronted with - and how can that spirit of novely persist while making adjustments for usability, accessiblity, and practicality?

No insights of value are gleamed solely from talking at another, a conversation is much better - people may not be willing to adapt their usability habits to avant-garde or indulgent design, so how can we integrate these qualities into their daily life, gently pushing at the constraints placed around our sense of novelty.
